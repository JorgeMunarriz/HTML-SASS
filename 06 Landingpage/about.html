KFC History

Kentucky Fried Chicken, officially known by its initials KFC, is an American fast food restaurant chain specialized in fried chicken, founded by Harland D. Sanders, who belongs to the group Yum! brands. Based in Louisville, Kentucky, has more than 22,000 restaurants in 150 countries and is located in the second position at the franchise level, behind McDonald's. The origin of the group dates back to 1939, when Harland D. Sanders began making crispy chicken in a restaurant in North Corbin (Kentucky) from a recipe with eleven spices and aromas. However, it was not until 1952 when opened the first KFC franchise in Salt Lake City, Utah. He Colonel Sanders controlled the company until 1964, when it was Sold to a local investor group. Later he went through the control of various companies such as R. J. Reynolds, until PepsiCo took over all the shares in 1986. Currently, the face of Harland Sanders continues to be linked to the restaurant chain as his corporate image.
